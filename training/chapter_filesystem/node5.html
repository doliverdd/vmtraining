man Pages

One of the most useful commands in Linux is the man command. This is the Linux help system. The man pages are broken up into NAME, SYNOPSIS, DESCRIPTION, OPTIONS, ENVIRONMENT, and SEE ALSO sections. There may be some additional sections, but these are the most common. The SYNOPSIS section gives the syntax of the command with [ ] denoting optional parameters. Under the DESCRIPTION section you will find a detail description of the action of the command in question. OPTIONS give a detailed look at each possible option for the command. In general Linux command options are preceded by a "-" or a "- -". The man page format becomes a very efficient help system once you become familiar with the layout.
