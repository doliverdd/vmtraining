Seminar Overview

The first day of the seminar will cover Unix/Linux basics. This section is intended as a bridge for the user that is only familiar with the various MS Windows operating systems. The topics will only be covered in an introductory manner. The second day will focus on how to download, use, and create scientific libraries. Day three and four consist of a parallel (excuse the unintended pun) introduction to MPI programming. On day three the attendees will look at this issue from a C programming perspective. On Day Four, the same topics will be covered but within the context of FORTRAN programming. The last day of the seminar will be in two parts. The morning will be spent discussing some of the modeling software available at RCC. The afternoon will be spent on discussions of the future of HPC at USF.
