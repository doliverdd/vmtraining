Compiling Basics and Make

Researchers must often write their own code, or at the very least modify code that someone else has written. In the *nix world this means working with one of the compilers that are available, and more often than not, working with a makefile.(or Makefile) This will be the topic of this sections. While we have several different compilers available on our systems we will concentrate on gcc for this section. Although different compilers have different options, the basic usage is the same. So by concentrating on one compiler we will avoid the complications of having too many options. The material should be easily transportable to other compilers, and even other languages.

Once programs reach a certain complexity, they may require certail libraries, include files, and may be contained in more than one file. The command to compile such a program may consist of several lines, and they may be rather long and often hard to type correctly on a consistent basis. These problems are further complicated by trying to remember which files where changed and what are the interdependencies. Fortunately, in the *nix world we have the command make. With a properly formated makefile, all of these problems are handled automatically.
